About

We love music!

Grown Women Dance Collective was founded in 2009 to provide a positive and nurturing environment for mature, female dancers to create beautiful work that is relevant and accessible to diverse audiences. GWDC creates cross cultural, cross generational bridges through the arts, introduces concert dance to new audiences and underserved communities, and challenges the stereotypes of aging. Grown Women Dance Collective is fiscally sponsored by 501c3


Tonya Marie Amos, Artistic Director


Tonya received a BA in Cultural Anthropology from U.C. Berkeley & trained four years on full scholarship at Alvin Ailey American Dance Center. A member of Actors Equity Association, she has appeared with Alvin Ailey American Dance Theater & Donald Byrd, was a member of Cleo Parker Robinson Dance Ensemble, Footprints & Amy Pivar Dances, performed in the International Tour of “West Side Story”, the National Tour of “Sesame St. Live”, Sacramento Music Circus’ “The King & I”, and has been featured in numerous print ads & TV commercials. She is the owner of Aspire Pilates Center in Concord for which she was awarded Pleasant Hill Chamber of Commerce’s “100 Women of Influence” in 2010, Women’s Initiative's Concord Female Entrepreneur of the Year in 2011, and Small Business of the Year 2013. The author of a monthly Health & Wellness column for the Pleasant Hill Community Focus newspaper, Tonya is currently putting the finishing touches on her Fall Prevention For Seniors dvd.

Marisa Castillo, Director of Design


Marisa Castillo loves to dance and has been dancing for as long as she can remember. In the mid-1980’s, she quickly found her focus on ballet, but secretly wished that she could pop, lock and break. She trained in classical ballet and learned the details and finesse of classical Vaganova technique with Karen Morell. Augusta Moore introduced Marisa to Feldenkrais and her unique approach to ballet which was life changing. Marisa has danced with Peninsula Ballet Theatre, Santa Clara Ballet, and Columbia City Ballet. She furthered her dancing in the Certificate Program at the Ailey School in New York. She currently teaches ballet at ODC Dance Commons in San Francisco, has taught at Stanford University, and is on the adjunct faculty at the University of San Francisco teaching Ballet.

Michelle Ned, Associate Artistic Director


Michelle Ned received a B.A. in Dance from SF State University, with emphasis in Performance and Choreography. She received intensive training in Graham, Horton and African Haitian techniques at Alvin Ailey, Garth Fagan and Katherine Dunham summer workshops. She has performed throughout the United States with Blanche Brown, Evelyn Thomas-Nuba Dance Theatre, and the Pearl Ubugen Musicians and Dancers. She has taught dance to children through the San Francisco Arts Education Foundation, Zohar Dance Studio, and Pacific Northwest Ballet, and has taught movement to seniors at the On Lok Adult Day Care Center in San Francisco. She has coached the San Francisco PAL Seahawks-Midget Dance Team, winning two National Championships. She is a director of the After School Youth Program of the Bayview YMCA in San Francisco.
